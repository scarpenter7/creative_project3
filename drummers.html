Drummers

Neil Peart

Many consider Neil Peart the greatest drummer who ever lived. He's famous for his 360 drum setup where he regularly spins in his swivel stool to make the craziest drum fills happen. His drumming style is incredibly precise and clean. His most iconic drum fills can be found in the songs "Tom Sawyer", "YYZ", and perhaps "Limelight". Rush was the first band that I ever saw live and one of the most impressionable moments was is 15 minute long drum solo where he ran a complete instrumental group completely on his drums. He would do percussion but also hook up different music loops to some of his electric drums to make it all happen. Neil is one of the reasons why I want to learn drums at some point in my life.

Drummers

Danny Carey

As drummer for TOOL, Danny brings a lot to the table. This guy holds down funky beats like nobody else. Somehow, he makes 5/4 time signatures sound normal. It's not until you manually count the beats to the song that you recognize that you're in an irregular time signature. When you hear his songs for the first time, it's often hard to pinpoint when he's going to lay down the snare or finish a particular drum fill. But every time he does it, it he nails it and controls the flow of the song. Some drummers are very technical and throw everything they can at their songs. Danny does that but in a way that accentuates the mood of the song. His use of electric drums is also very clever. He doesn't use it to add flash, but rather to complement the song.
